How it works

A booth that runs itself.

  1. 01

    You give me a corner.

    A compact analog station drops into your space: a few square feet, power, done. I design it to fit the room, not fight it.

  2. 02

    I run everything.

    Photography, the guest flow, payments, and on-the-spot production. Nothing for your team to staff, set up, or babysit.

  3. 03

    Guests leave with a keepsake.

    Real borders, real film-like texture, your venue on the print. They carry it home, post it, and remember exactly where they got it.

Who's behind it

Run by someone who studies nostalgia for a living.

I'm Josh Gonzales, a PhD candidate at the University of Guelph. Nostalgia is one of the things I actually research, so future.nostalgia isn't a franchise or a faceless rental. It's me behind the camera at every booth, running the night start to finish.

I started this for my own family. The moments that matter most are the ones you can hold onto for the long run, so the whole idea rests on a simple finding: a photo you can keep becomes a memory you keep. That's what I want every guest to walk away with.

A share of profits goes to supporting brain research. The science of memory is what makes a photograph worth keeping in the first place.
