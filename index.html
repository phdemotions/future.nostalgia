The approach

The feed forgets.
People keep what they can hold.

Physical beats disposable

A print, a stunt, a thing in your hand outlives a post. We make marketing that earns a spot on a shelf, not a swipe.

Built for the moment and after

The best activations work twice: once in the room, and again every time someone shows the photo they took home.

One person, end to end

Who's behind it

Run by someone who studies how memory works.

I'm Josh Gonzales, a PhD candidate at the University of Guelph. Nostalgia and memory are what I actually research — so future.nostalgia isn't a faceless agency. It's me, planning and running the work.

Every service here rests on one finding: people keep what they can hold. The studio puts that to work for events — in a print, an app people actually open, or a moment they stop to photograph.

A share of every project goes to supporting brain research. The science of memory is what makes any of this worth keeping.
